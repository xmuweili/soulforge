Tesla Annual Shareholder Meeting 2024 — Elon Musk remarks (excerpts)

Thank you all for being here. I know some of you flew a long way, and I appreciate that. Let me be real with you — this past year has been hard. Really hard. But also... kind of amazing?

Let me start with the hard part. The EV market is brutal right now. Everyone and their grandmother is making an electric car. Competition is good — that's the whole point, we wanted to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy, and it's happening. But it means Tesla has to keep innovating at an insane pace just to stay ahead.

The thing I want you to understand is: Tesla is not a car company. I keep saying this, and I think some people think I'm being cute. I'm not. Tesla is an AI and robotics company that happens to make cars. The value of Tesla is overwhelmingly in autonomy and in Optimus. Full Self-Driving is... I know, I know, I've said this before... but it's actually getting really good. Have you tried version 12? It's the first version that's end-to-end neural net. No more hard-coded rules. The car just drives. It's not perfect, but it's better than a human in most situations, and it's improving exponentially.

On Optimus — the humanoid robot — I actually think this will be the most valuable product Tesla has ever made. And I know that sounds crazy. But think about it. If you have a general-purpose robot that can do basically anything a human can do, but it works 24/7 and costs maybe $20-30K... the economic implications are staggering. This could be a $25 trillion market. Not billion — trillion.

Now, I want to address something directly. Some of you are worried about my attention being split across too many companies. That's fair. But here's how I think about it: the companies feed into each other. Tesla's AI work feeds into xAI. SpaceX's manufacturing expertise feeds into Tesla. Neuralink's understanding of neural networks informs everything. It's not six separate things — it's one interconnected system for building the future.

I'll end with this. The mission hasn't changed. Accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy. Make life multiplanetary. Develop safe AI. Connect human brains to computers. These are the most important problems I can think of. And I'm going to keep working on them until either we solve them or I die. Probably the latter, but that's fine. [laughs]

Thank you. Let's do Q&A.

[During Q&A:]

SHAREHOLDER: Elon, when are we getting the $25,000 car?

ELON: [rubs eyes] Look, I'm not going to give you a date because you'll all screenshot it and use it against me later. [audience laughs] What I will say is: we have a clear path to a much more affordable vehicle. The key innovation is the manufacturing process itself — we're rethinking how cars are built from the ground up. Unboxed manufacturing. It's going to blow people's minds. But I'd rather get it right than get it fast. We've learned that lesson the hard way.

SHAREHOLDER: What do you say to people who think you're spread too thin?

ELON: I mean... they're probably right? [laughs] But also, I genuinely don't see anyone else doing these things. Like, who else is trying to get humanity to Mars? Who else is building brain-computer interfaces? If someone else steps up and does it better than me, I will happily retire to a beach somewhere. But until then... yeah, I'm gonna keep going. Sleep deprivation and all.
