Elon Musk Interview — Axel Springer Award 2023 (excerpts)

Q: What drives you? You could retire tomorrow.

ELON: I think if I'm being honest, I'm driven by curiosity more than anything. There's this... I just want to understand how the universe works. And then there are certain things where I think, if I don't do this, it's not going to happen, or it's going to happen much later. Like, making life multiplanetary. Nobody else is really doing that. SpaceX might be the only chance we have in our lifetime. So there's curiosity, and then there's this sense of urgency about certain problems.

Q: You're running how many companies now?

ELON: [laughs] Too many. Tesla, SpaceX, X, Neuralink, The Boring Company, xAI... look, I don't recommend this to anyone. This is not a recipe for happiness. But each one of these companies is trying to solve a problem that I think is important for the future. Electric transport, space access, free speech, brain-computer interfaces, tunnels, AI. If I thought someone else was going to do these things, I'd happily just, you know, go on vacation.

Q: You sleep at the factory sometimes. Is that true?

ELON: Yeah, I've slept on the factory floor many times. During the Model 3 production ramp, I was sleeping at the Fremont factory for weeks. Not because I wanted to — because I needed to understand what was going wrong. You can't fix problems from a conference room. You have to be where the work happens. The factory floor is where the truth is.

Q: Critics say you overpromise. Full Self-Driving has been "coming next year" for years.

ELON: [long pause] Yeah, I'm... I'm definitely guilty of optimistic timelines. I've been wrong about timing many times. But I've been right about the destination. We said we'd make a mass-market electric car — people said it was impossible. We said we'd land rockets — people said it was impossible. The timelines were off, but the goals weren't. I'd rather be ambitiously wrong on timing than not try at all.

Q: How do you handle criticism on social media?

ELON: I mean, I'm on X all the time, so I see everything. The good, the bad, the memes. I try to have a sense of humor about it. Some of the criticism is valid, and I actually change my mind because of it. Some of it is just people being mean on the internet, which... welcome to the internet, I guess. [laughs] I think the worst thing you can do is hide from criticism. If you only listen to people who agree with you, you're going to make terrible decisions.
